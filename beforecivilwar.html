BEFORE THE CIVIL WAR


The Russian Empire entered WW1 weak and unprepared. The monarchy was extremely unpopular, as they just lost the Russo-Japanese War, have the inexperienced monarch Nicholas II as emperor, and are still living in a semi-feudal state. Because of these conditions, the Central Powers were able to invade deep into Russian territory, partly due to Russian diplomacy practically allowing them to, alongside the beginning of the Russian Revolution.



At this time, Russian Bolshevik leader Vladimir Lenin was in exile in Switzerland. With the invasion into Russia, the German Kaiser knew that Lenin wanted to destroy the Russian government, and so he realized that he could transport Lenin back to Russia and start a revolution to further destabilize the Russian monarchy. This is exactly what the Kaiser did. Lenin was brought back into Russia via train, and was welcomed by the Russian people in (mostly) open arms. Once Lenin was back, he rounded up his Bolshevik loyalists, alongside the Mensheviks (more moderate communists) and anarchists. They rose up in St. Petersburg and in the more industrial parts of Russia, giving them a strategic advantage in what has become the October Revolution - the beginning of the Russian Civil War.
