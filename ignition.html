IGNITION OF THE CIVIL WAR


The Russian Civil War would officially begin November 7, 1917. Due to the heightened unrest of the population, the power vacuum created by Nicholas II's abdication, the rising Bolshevik government, and further invasions by foreign powers, the pot of instability finally exploded.

Figures such as Lenin, Stalin, and Trotsky were the main representatives for the Bolshevik movements, and figures like Kornilov, Kolchak, Wrangel, and Ungern von Sternberg were representatives of the White Army.

The Bolshevik government rose up in the Russian capital of Petrograd (now St. Petersburg) and other industrialized areas of Russia, giving them a huge advantage in the civil war. However, for the White Army, they controlled much of the more rural territory and lands with different, distinct ethnic groups, which created much conflict. Based on these conditions, it is no surprise to see why the Reds had much more success in what was to come.
